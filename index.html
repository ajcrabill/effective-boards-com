Five Governance Fundamentals — Regardless of Sector

  • The board's job is outcomes — not operations, not activities, not plans. Outcomes.
  • What the board monitors is what the organization prioritizes. If student learning (or mission achievement, or patient outcomes) isn't monitored, it isn't the priority — no matter what the strategic plan says.
  • The board and the CEO/Executive Director need a clear boundary. Blurring it makes both parties less effective.
  • Every governance process should be traceable back to an outcome it serves. If it isn't, it shouldn't be in the process.
  • Accountability isn't punishment. It's measurement. A board that can't measure outcomes can't govern.
