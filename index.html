Five Governance Fundamentals — Regardless of Sector

  • 1 The board's job is outcomes — not operations, not activities, not plans. Outcomes. Everything else is in service of that, or it isn't governance.
  • 2 What the board monitors is what the organization prioritizes. If mission achievement isn't being monitored, it isn't the priority — regardless of what the strategic plan says.
  • 3 The board and the chief executive need a clear boundary. Blurring it makes both parties less effective and obscures accountability for outcomes.
  • 4 Every governance process should be traceable back to an outcome it serves. If it isn't, it has no place in the governance system.
  • 5 Accountability isn't punishment — it's measurement. A board that cannot measure outcomes cannot govern. Full stop.
